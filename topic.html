"ENVIROMENTAL SUSTAINABILITY"

PROTECT NATURE (environmental sustainability) Pakistan is vulnerability personified when it comes to environmental climate change. Rising temperatures, altered precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events imperil the country's fragile ecosystem. The 2022 floods, which affected 33 million people and caused $30 billion in damages, exemplify the devastating consequences of climate change. Pakistan's glaciers, which supply 75% of its freshwater, are melting at an alarming rate, threatening water scarcity and agricultural productivity. Heatwaves, like the 2015 Karachi tragedy that claimed 1,200 lives, are becoming more frequent. Deforestation, industrial pollution, and unsustainable agricultural practices exacerbate these issues. Climate migration, food insecurity, and health crises loom large. To mitigate these impacts, Pakistan must prioritize renewable energy, reforestation, climate-resilient infrastructure, and adaptive agricultural practices. International cooperation, climate finance, and grassroots activism are essential for safeguarding Pakistan's ecological integrity and ensuring a livable future.

3. ⁠*Billion Tree Tsunami:* Pakistan's largest reforestation project, launched in 2014, aimed to plant 1 billion trees across Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. The project achieved its goal in 2018, increasing forest cover, reducing greenhouse gas emissions, and creating jobs.

Lahore Waste Management Project: Lahore's first integrated waste management system, initiated in 2019, collects and processes 3,000 tons of waste daily, reducing landfill waste by 70% and generating electricity.

⁠*Pakistan's First Electric Vehicle Policy:* Introduced in 2020, this policy aims to promote electric vehicles, reducing emissions and dependence on fossil fuels. Incentives include tax exemptions, subsidies, and investment in EV infrastructure.

Indus River Dolphin Conservation:* WWF-Pakistan's conservation efforts, launched in 2016, protected the endangered Indus River dolphin. Habitat preservation, fishing regulations, and community engagement led to a 50% population increase.

*Green Pakistan Programme:* Launched in 2017, this national initiative focuses on reforestation, wildlife conservation, and eco-tourism development. It aims to plant 100 million trees, protect wildlife habitats, and promote sustainable livelihoods.
